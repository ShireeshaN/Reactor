Quality & Safety

Engineered with Responsibility. Executed with Discipline.

At Reactor Care, quality and safety are not optional standards — they are core operational principles.

Industrial reactors operate under:

  • High pressure
  • High temperature
  • Aggressive chemicals
  • Continuous production cycles

Safety First Approach

Any technical negligence can lead to equipment damage, contamination, production loss, or safety hazards. Every service follows a structured quality and safety framework.

  • Complete equipment isolation
  • Internal cleaning confirmation
  • Safe entry conditions
  • Structural stability verification
  • Controlled dismantling procedures
  • Safe lifting and handling methods

Pre-Service Technical Inspection

Every project begins with detailed technical evaluation:

  • Glass lining condition assessment (GLR)
  • Shaft alignment inspection
  • Agitator & impeller condition check
  • Seal performance evaluation
  • Bearing and coupling inspection
  • Jacket and nozzle examination

We do not proceed without proper diagnosis.

Glass-Lined Reactor (GLR) Protection Standards

  • Controlled spark testing
  • Damage mapping & classification
  • Impact prevention near glass surfaces
  • Proper handling of nozzles & internals
  • Post-service verification testing

Our goal is long-term corrosion resistance and structural integrity.

Precision Mechanical Standards

  • Proper shaft alignment
  • Coupling concentricity check
  • Torque-controlled fastening
  • Impeller balance verification
  • Seal installation with compatibility confirmation

Precision execution reduces recurring failures and vibration issues.

Testing & Validation

  • Spark testing (GLR)
  • Hydro testing (if required)
  • Leak detection
  • Operational rotation check
  • Vibration observation
  • Final inspection review

No reactor is considered complete without validation.
